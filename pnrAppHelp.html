Boston Region Metropolitan Planning Organization (MPO) Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority (MBTA) Park-and- Ride and Bicycle Parking Dashboard

MBTA Park and Ride and Bicycle Parking Monitoring - Purpose

The monitoring of MBTA park-and-ride and bicycle parking is part of a larger effort of the Congestion Management Process (CMP). The CMP is a federally mandated program that requires a multimodal approach to monitoring traffic congestion. The Boston Region MPO’s CMP monitors expressways, arterials, high-occupancy vehicle lanes, park-and-ride facilities, bicycle parking facilities, and bicycle and pedestrian transportation infrastructure. This dashboard focuses on monitoring the daily presence of parking near 152 MBTA stations, and bicycle parking near 280 MBTA stations. For additional information, including a detailed analysis of the systemwide park-and-ride and bicycle parking results, please click on the link to the memoranda below.

Using the Application

The main screen displays a default map of the Boston region.

To use the interface:

  1. There is a choice to view the basemap using either MassGIS or Open Street Map Layer. Click the respective desired circle.
  2. Click the “Select a town or neighborhood” drop-down menu to narrow down the area of the desired MBTA station. Note, while this is not necessary, every MBTA station will be displayed if a neighborhood or town is not selected on the next tab. Once a neighborhood or town is selected, the MBTA station options are narrowed down to the specific neighborhood.
  3. Click the “Select a MBTA station” drop-down tab to select a single MBTA station for viewing. The map will zoom in to the selected station, and detailed data will be displayed below the map in the travel network.
  4. The “Station information” tab, located below the map, will display detailed data about the selected MBTA station. This includes bicycle parking data and features, such as bicycle trails, bicycle lanes, crosswalks, sidewalks, and signals near the MBTA stations.
  5. The “Parking information” tab, located towards the bottom of the dashboard, will display detailed data for every parking lot that MBTA commuters typically use near the MBTA station.

The bicycle parking data for 2017–18 can be downloaded in Excel format by clicking the Download Stations and Bike Data button. The parking data near MBTA stations can be downloaded in Excel format by clicking the Download Parking Lot Data button.

MBTA Park-and-Ride Lot Column Definitions

Station Name: Name of MBTA station.

Lot ID: Numerical identification of a specific parking lot near station, as defined by the MPO (some stations may have more than one parking lot, however, each parking lot has a unique parking lot ID).

Line ID: Common name of the transit line that serves the MBTA station.

Mode: Mode of transportation that the station serves (commuter rail, rapid transit, bus rapid transit, bus, ferry).

Public Parking Non-Accessible Spaces: Any parking spaces at the specified parking lot that are not designated to be accessible, and do not require a permit.

Occupied Public Parking Non-Accessible Spaces: Any occupied parking spaces at the specified parking lot that are not designated to be accessible, and do not require a permit.

Utilization of Public Parking Non-Accessible Spaces: The percent of all parking spaces in use compared to the total number of spaces that are available to the public, which are non- accessible at the specified parking lot.

Non-Accessible Parking Spaces: Any parking spaces at the specified parking lot that are not designated to be accessible. This includes both public parking and permit only spaces.

Occupied Non-Accessible Parking Spaces: Any occupied parking spaces at the specified parking lot that are not designated to be accessible. This includes occupied public parking and permit only spaces.

Accessible Parking Spaces: Total number of accessible parking spaces at the parking lot.

Occupied Accessible Parking Spaces: Total number of vehicles parked in accessible parking spaces at the parking lot at the time of the survey.

All Total Parking Spaces: Any parking spaces that are present at a specified parking lot. This includes public and permit only, and accessible and non-accessible spaces.

All Total Occupied Spaces: Any occupied parking spaces at the specified parking lot. This includes public and permit only, and accessible and non-accessible spaces.

Utilization of All Parking Spaces: The percent of all parking spaces in use at a designated parking lot compared to the total number of parking spaces that are available. This includes public and permit only, and accessible and non-accessible spaces.

Cars Not in Marked Spaces: Total number of vehicles that are parked in unmarked/undesignated parking spaces in parking lot.

Lot Ownership: The owner of the parking lot (MBTA or private).

Parking Fee: Daily or monthly cost to park a vehicle in the parking lot. Permit only is indicated for parking lots that require a residential or private permit.

Station Information and Bicycle Parking Information Column Definitions

Year(s) Collected: Time period in which data was collected for the MBTA station.

Line: MBTA transit line that the station resides on.

ST Num: Designated identification number for the MBTA station.

ST Code: Designated code for MBTA station.

Station Mode: Mode of transportation that the MBTA station serves (commuter rail, rapid transit, bus rapid transit, bus, ferry).

Number of Bicycle Spaces: Total observed number of occupied and empty bicycle spaces that are present at a MBTA station. The number of bicycle spaces are determined by the number and type of bicycle racks present at the MBTA station.

Number of Bicycles Parked: Total number of bicycles parked exclusively at racks at a MBTA station during the time of observation. This figure does not include bicycles parked at objects other than bicycle racks near MBTA stations.

Bicycle Rack Types Present: Type of bicycle racks present at a MBTA station.

How Many Other Locations: Total number of bicycles parked at objects other than bicycle racks near MBTA stations.

Bike Trail Nearby: Indicates if there is a bicycle trail near the MBTA station.

Bike Lanes Leading to Station: Indicates if bicycle lanes are present on any roadways leading to the MBTA station.

Sidewalks Leading to Station: Indicates if there is a sidewalk leading to the MBTA station.

Sidewalk Condition: Indicates the condition of the sidewalks leading to the MBTA station.

Crosswalk Leading to Station: Indicates if there is a crosswalk leading to the MBTA station.

Crosswalk Condition: Indicates the condition of any crosswalks leading to the MBTA station.

Signal near Station: Indicates if there is a traffic signal near the MBTA station.

Pedestrian Signal near Station: Indicates if there is a pedestrian signal near the MBTA station.

Data Sources/CMP Standards

All information presented by this dashboard was collected by Boston Region MPO staff during a one day evaluation for each station. Each station was evaluated in 2017 or 2018.

Technical Requirements

This application requires a W3C standards-compliant Web browser (Microsoft Edge, Google Chrome, Internet Explorer, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, and Opera).
